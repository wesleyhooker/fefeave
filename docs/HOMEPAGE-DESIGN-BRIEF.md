# Homepage design brief — Figma exploration

Concise brief for a homepage visual exploration. Pick one direction (or blend) for a future Figma pass. No implementation in this doc.

---

## Direction A: **Warm minimal**

- **Visual personality:** Calm, approachable, “small business you can trust.” Plenty of whitespace; no visual noise. Feels like a friendly shop window, not a startup landing page.
- **Color palette:** Warm neutrals (stone/cream) with one soft accent (e.g. terracotta or sage). Gray-900 for text; avoid cold grays. Optional: very light warm tint on section backgrounds.
- **Typography feel:** Serif or soft sans for headings (e.g. Lora, Source Serif, or a rounded sans); clean sans for body. Slightly larger body size for readability. No heavy or techy fonts.
- **Button/card style:** Rounded corners (e.g. rounded-xl), subtle borders or soft shadows. Buttons: filled primary in the warm accent; secondary with border. Cards: light border, optional soft shadow.
- **Best for:** Emphasizing trust and personality; resellers and wholesale partners; “human” brand. Fits “Fefe Ave is a small, owner-led shop.”
- **Recommendation:** Strong fit if the main audience is wholesalers and long-term partners who value clarity and a personal touch.

---

## Direction B: **Clean product**

- **Visual personality:** Polished, “finished SaaS.” Clear hierarchy, consistent spacing, professional but not corporate. Feels like a tool you’d pay for.
- **Color palette:** Neutral gray base (slate or blue-gray); single accent (e.g. one blue or green) for CTAs and key elements. High contrast for text; muted for secondary.
- **Typography feel:** One clear sans (e.g. Inter, DM Sans, or Geist). Tight heading tracking; comfortable body line-height. Clear scale (e.g. 2xl/4xl hero, xl section, base body).
- **Button/card style:** rounded-lg or rounded-xl; cards with border-gray-200 and shadow-sm. Primary: solid dark or accent; secondary: outline. Hover/focus states obvious.
- **Best for:** Positioning FefeAve as a serious operations tool; mixed audience (Felicia + future teams). Scales well if the product grows.
- **Recommendation:** Best if the goal is “this looks like a real product” and you want a single system that works for both marketing and app UI.

---

## Direction C: **Live & social**

- **Visual personality:** Energetic, “where the action is.” References live sales, community, and discovery. Slightly bolder than A or B but still readable.
- **Color palette:** Dark or light hero option; one vivid accent (e.g. coral, electric blue, or green) for CTAs and highlights. Strong contrast; optional gradient or texture in hero only.
- **Typography feel:** Bold sans for hero (e.g. font-semibold or font-bold); distinctive but legible. Body stays clean. Optional: one display touch (e.g. “Live sales” in a heavier weight).
- **Button/card style:** Rounded, possibly pill for primary CTA. Cards: clear borders or light shadows; optional hover lift. Links and CTAs feel “clickable.”
- **Best for:** Driving traffic to live shows and Whatnot/Instagram; younger or highly social audience. Good if the homepage is mainly a funnel to live and shop.
- **Recommendation:** Use if the main job of the homepage is to get people to join lives or follow; tone down slightly so it doesn’t feel like a generic “growth” landing page.

---

## Recommendation summary

- **Default pick:** **Direction B (Clean product)** — one system for homepage and app, clear hierarchy, minimal risk, easy to maintain.
- **If brand should feel more “human” and trust-first:** **Direction A (Warm minimal)**.
- **If the homepage is primarily a funnel to live/social:** **Direction C (Live & social)** with restraint (e.g. one bold hero, rest clean).

Use this brief to kick off 3 short Figma explorations (hero + one section each), then choose or blend before implementation.
